Designing an India-first 40B

A proposal for a 40B dense, multimodal model that must match Gemma 4 on reasoning, code and agents, while being measurably better for India's languages, institutions and daily work.

15Tscheduled tokens
28%direct India data
200,192token vocabulary
256Ktarget context

“India-first” is a coverage and evaluation constraint, not one official or majoritarian point of view.

NASA/GSFC, India night lights 2012–2016

The decision

Train only after 1–3B proxy ablations validate the mix. Require 7.5T clean unique-equivalent tokens, no document more than four passes, and a matched-harness lower confidence bound within 2 points of Gemma 4 31B. If the data or gates fail, the 40B run does not start or ship.

02 / Data pipeline

Clean for the objective, not for English

One immutable ledger follows every object from acquisition through deletion: source, rights, time, hashes, language/script, transformations, quality, region, synthetic lineage and opt-out ID.

  1. 1
    Rights first

    Public-domain, permissive or contracted. Reject piracy and unresolved books/news; allow-list code licenses per file.

  2. 2
    Parse by format

    DOM and PDF reading order, OCR confidence, notebook cells, tables and LaTeX. Preserve lists, citations and tests.

  3. 3
    Language-aware quality

    Paragraph-level language + script ID; native-rater-calibrated filters. Keep genuine romanization, dialect and code-switching.

  4. 4
    Deduplicate before sampling

    SHA-256, repeated spans, global MinHash, translation clusters and repo/fork/AST dedup. Store cluster size.

  5. 5
    Privacy + secrets

    Redact Aadhaar, PAN, bank, health, phone and address data; remove API keys, credentials, private messages and doxxing.

  6. 6
    Balance false positives

    Remove illegal/exploitative payloads, not caste, sexuality, political criticism or dialect. Audit removals per community and language.

  7. 7
    Contamination firewall

    Freeze evals first; remove prompts and answers via exact, 13-gram, MinHash, semantic, translated and code-AST matches.

  8. 8
    Verify + snapshot

    Execute code/agents; check math, names and numbers; cap all synthetic exposure at 12%; publish data cards and deletion logs.

03 / Tokenizer

200,192 entries

Recent 22-language ablations place the useful knee near 200K. At an 8,192 hidden width, a tied vocabulary costs 1.64B parameters; Gemma 4's 262K would cost 2.15B. Spend the difference on the transformer.

04 / Post-training + RL

Use human judgment where truth is subjective

Use executable rewards where truth is verifiable. A learned reward can contribute at most 20% and can never overrule a failing unit test, symbolic check, tool policy or unauthorized side effect.

What SFT teaches

650K India/Indic tasks; 650K code/repository work; 600K tool and browser/terminal/office trajectories; 500K math/science; 450K grounded helpfulness; 200K long context/RAG; 150K safety and correction.

Natural-language SFT is 45% in 12 core Indic languages, 10% in the remaining scheduled languages, 10% Hinglish/code-switch and 35% English. At least 25% of code and agent prompts are Indic or Hinglish.

What RL rewards

Task correctness 55%; schema/instruction compliance 15%; grounding 10%; efficiency 10%; language quality 10%. Critical unsafe or unauthorized action ends the episode at −1. Reward the final state, not a persuasive-looking chain of thought.

Before use, each judge/reward model needs per-language ROC-AUC ≥0.80, calibration error ≤0.05 and human weighted κ ≥0.65. Audit 5% of rollouts for reward hacking.

Constitutional pluralism

Dignity, equality, liberty, secular pluralism, privacy and child safety. For contested history, religion, caste, borders or politics: date and jurisdiction, separate fact/law/opinion, attribute credible perspectives and uncertainty. Include Dalit, Adivasi, minority-religion, LGBTQ+, disability, rural/urban and regional annotators; retain disagreement.

05 / Evaluation contract

Parity outside India; superiority inside it

Freeze the training cutoff and benchmark registry. Run the same harness, prompts, tools and sampling against Gemma 4 31B. Three seeds, bootstrap 95% CIs, worst-language reporting and no launch on a critical failure.
